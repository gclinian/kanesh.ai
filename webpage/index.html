The problem

The most valuable training data in chip design is locked away.

Data providers can't share

Foundries hold billions of dollars of PDK, production netlists, layout, and PPA data. Fabless leaders hold battle-tested RTL and verification telemetry. Moving any of it outside a secure perimeter is a legal, strategic, and national-security non-starter.

AI developers can't train

ML-for-EDA startups and academic labs train on toy benchmarks that bear little resemblance to 3nm production. The result: models that never close the loop to a real tape-out, and an industry that leaves massive PPA improvements on the table.

Today, the most economically valuable dataset in chip design is also the least usable. Kanesh changes that — without asking anyone to trust anyone.

The Kanesh stack

Three cryptographic layers. One trustless platform.

Kanesh composes three independent defense-in-depth layers so that a malicious coordinator, a curious peer, and a compromised network are all insufficient to leak a single line of RTL.

01

Training happens where the data lives. Kanesh ships EDA-native architectures — graph neural networks over netlists, Transformers over RTL, U-Nets over layout heatmaps — and orchestrates rounds across foundry and fabless sites with Byzantine-robust aggregation.

02

Privacy Shield

Four layers. Zero assumptions.

Differential Privacy (DP-SGD), Secure Aggregation, and confidential-compute TEEs compose so that even a fully compromised coordinator learns nothing about individual contributors. Gradient-inversion and membership-inference attacks are neutralized independently at each layer.

  • NVIDIA H100 Confidential Compute
  • Intel TDX · AMD SEV-SNP
  • DP-SGD · Bonawitz Secure Aggregation
03

Contributors paid in proportion to value.

Data Shapley quantifies each dataset's marginal utility to the trained model. Contributions translate into standard enterprise SaaS revenue shares, with cryptographic proofs verifying training integrity and a transparent dispute-resolution process handled by independent auditors.

  • Data Shapley · KNN-Shapley
  • Cryptographic proof of training integrity

How it works

From a training job to a settled reward — in four steps.

  1. 1

    Register & stake

    Data providers publish a cryptographically attested dataset card. Developers deposit bounties. Identities are bound to hardware attestation keys.

  2. 2

    Train locally

    Each round, the coordinator ships model weights to enclaves on the provider premises. Gradients are computed under DP, masked with secure-aggregation shares, and attested by TEEs before leaving the site.

  3. 3

    Aggregate & attest

    Byzantine-robust aggregation produces a new global model. A cryptographic proof attests that the training followed the agreed protocol. Commitments to every gradient are written to a tamper-evident audit log.

  4. 4

    Settle fairly

    Data Shapley ranks contributors by the marginal utility they brought. Contributions translate into standard enterprise SaaS revenue shares, distributed via conventional invoicing. Any authorized party can audit the ledger.
